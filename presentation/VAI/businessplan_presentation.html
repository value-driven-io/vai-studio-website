A Paradise at a Crossroads

French Polynesia's tourism generates 99 billion XPF annually and employs 1 in 5 workers. But behind this polished surface lies a system in crisis.

The Paradise Paradox
While tourists pay premium prices for an "untouched paradise," local entrepreneurs surrender 20-30% of their revenue to foreign platforms, communities face a 16% poverty rate, and the government lacks real-time data to manage this vital 99 billion XPF economy.

Stakeholder Ecosystem

Understanding the Complex Web of Interests

Five Critical Tensions
Our analysis reveals five systemic tensions: the Myth vs Reality gap, land ownership paralysis, public-private labor competition, authenticity commodification, and inter-archipelago inequality.

Public Sector Stakeholders

Government, Tahiti Tourisme, Service du Tourisme

Core Frustrations

  • The Data Black Hole: No real-time data for governing the 99B XPF industry effectively
  • Conflicting Strategies: FM27 sustainable growth (280k) vs. ambitious targets (600k tourists)
  • Digitalization Gap: 4,500+ small operators excluded from formal digital economy
  • Policy Implementation: Difficulty executing national strategies at local level

Desired Outcomes

  • Evidence-Based Governance: Real-time tourism satellite account for informed decisions
  • FM27 Success: Inclusive growth, experience diversification, workforce professionalization
  • Economic Self-Sufficiency: Reduced dependency on French financial transfers
  • Digital Sovereignty: Local control over tourism data and infrastructure

Local Entrepreneurs

Activity Providers & Pension Owners - The Heart of Authenticity

The Margin Squeeze

  • Financial Disempowerment: 20-30% commission to Viator, GetYourGuide, Expedia
  • Payment Chaos: 30-60 day delays, USD/EUR volatility, currency conversion losses
  • Operational Burden: Multiple booking channels causing costly double-bookings
  • Digital Invisibility: Cannot compete with billion-dollar OTA advertising budgets

Desired Liberation

  • Direct Market Access: Profitable, sustainable businesses with fair commissions
  • Financial Stability: Fast XPF payouts, predictable cash flow
  • Operational Simplicity: Single calendar managing all booking channels
  • Level Playing Field: Compete on experience quality, not marketing budget

Private Sector Stakeholders

DMCs, International Hotels, Airlines

Growth Constraints

  • Supply Bottleneck: 74.5% hotel occupancy with limited room capacity expansion
  • Policy Uncertainty: Conflicting government strategies create investment risk
  • Disintermediation Threat: Direct booking platforms threaten DMC business models
  • Operational Inefficiency: Fragmented booking systems and poor data coordination

Strategic Evolution

  • Stable Investment Climate: Predictable policy environment for long-term planning
  • Value Chain Enhancement: DMCs evolve into high-value experience curators
  • Operational Excellence: Integrated systems reducing cancellations and inefficiencies
  • Data Partnership: Strategic collaboration through anonymized market insights

The Local Community

Workforce, Cultural Custodians, Residents - The Foundation

Social License Erosion

  • Economic Exclusion: Glass ceiling in tourism jobs, 16% poverty despite boom
  • Inequitable Burden: Communities bear tourism costs without proportional benefits
  • Cultural Commodification: Fear of authentic traditions becoming superficial performances
  • Decision Exclusion: Top-down planning without community voice in development

Co-constructed Future

  • Inclusive Prosperity: Quality jobs and entrepreneurial opportunities for Polynesians
  • Community Partnership: Genuine voice in tourism planning and governance
  • Cultural Preservation: Tourism that protects and celebrates authentic traditions
  • Tangible Benefits: Visible improvements in community well-being and infrastructure

The Repatriation Mechanism
Our 11% commission immediately returns 10-20% more revenue to local operators on every booking. The 5% social impact contribution creates a virtuous cycle where tourism growth directly funds the preservation of the cultural and environmental assets it depends on.

Partnership Doctrine

Responsible Public-Private Governance

Governance Board

Representatives from the Presidency, Ministry of Tourism, Tahiti Tourisme, Service du Tourisme, and VAI ensure strategic alignment and shared decision-making.

Data Sovereignty

All raw tourism data remains the sovereign property of the Government of French Polynesia. VAI licenses anonymized, aggregated data while the core asset stays with the nation.

Operator Advisory Council

Quarterly meetings with local entrepreneurs provide powerful community voice to guide platform evolution and ensure grassroots needs are met.
