Sizing the Opportunity

From the massive global travel industry down to a specific, achievable target in French Polynesia, here's how we define the market for VAI.

Step 1: The Global Context (TAM)

The big picture is clear: the world loves to travel. The global tourism market is worth trillions, and key trends perfectly match what French Polynesia offers.

The Adventure Tourism market (over $400 billion) and the Luxury Travel market (over $1.4 trillion) are growing fast. These are travelers looking for the exact kind of unique, high-quality experiences that local Polynesian operators provide.

The biggest shift in travel is the move away from pre-packaged tours. More than ever, people want to plan their own trips and find authentic, local experiences. This creates a huge demand for a platform that connects them directly with the small operators who are currently hard to find.

Step 2: The Local Market (SAM)

Now, let's focus on French Polynesia. It's a high-value destination where tourists stay longer and spend more, creating a rich market for activities.

In 2024, over 263,000 tourists visited, with the government aiming for 600,000 soon. Each tourist spends around $3,400 during their trip (an average of 16 days), making the total economic impact massive.

Step 3: VAI's Target Market (SOM)

To get a realistic target, we focus on the right type of tourist and assume a conservative market share to start.

Our primary target isn't cruise passengers, who have limited time and book through the cruise line. Our focus is on the 80% of tourists who stay on the islands. They stay longer (16 days on average) and actively plan and book their own activities, facing all the frustrations VAI is designed to solve.
